Mission

Our mission is to reduce the risk factor currently facing the Somali Bantu Youth’s in San Diego, California.

Vision Statement

Our purpose is to:

  1. Empower and enhance the Somali Bantu Youth of San Diego
  2. Help the Somali Bantu youths attain secondary, post secondary, technical, vocational, and other types of education
  3. Promote social and emotional self-reliance
  4. Provide and facilitate training for the Somali Bantu youths to ensure the development of various life skills, gain employment, community opportunities, and develop strength.

Youth Program: Beyond the Horizon

Who We Are

The Somali Bantu Youth’s soccer team is a volunteer no-profit organization designed to offer a fun and competitive environment for the youths. We are also geared to maintain a drug and violence free environment

Our Goal:

  1. To provide an affordable venue that promotes interaction between youth and young adults of various communities and ages, especially those considered at risk (defined) as minorities (with low income)
  2. To provide a form in which youths can be mentored in terms of spiritual and physical knowledge by respectful individuals
  3. To provide a positive environment and respite for youths from negative peer pressures, criminal propensities, dysfunctional families, and other environmental or sourced-economic factors
  4. Volunteer opportunities
  5. Providing youths and young adults the responsibilities and skills that they can develop to achieve success and confidence in the future endeavors

Our Objective:

To fulfill a community need by promoting a sense of relationship among youths and young adults of both Somali Bantu and non-Somali Bantu backgrounds in the context of multicultural athletic events. Using multicultural athletic events is relevant to achieve these goals as it promotes social interaction, cultural integration, team-work, co-operations, communication, and a sense of accomplishment amongst the participants.

Future Services:

In the future, the organization plans to increase and expand its current services to add new and better programs such as: training to manage and budget money, as the Somali Bantu youths may not be familiar with these skills. The organization will maintain programs that will (based on needs of the communities) develop, help and courage, provide a good future for Somali Bantu.

Sports:

The Somali Bantu Soccer Team (SBST) – The Somali Bantu youths love to play soccer! We have a team to motivate the youth, and hope to provide indoor games during other seasons. During the summer, the SBST will compete with other teams in neighboring states such as Seattle, Oregan, Ohio, Nevada, Texas, Arizona, Minnesota, etc. to help the young Somali Bantu youth interact with other youths.

Traditional Counseling and Mentoring Services:

The Somali Bantu youth meet many stressful situations in Somalia which negatively affects their progress with education. We as the Somali Bantu Youth Association of America, provide counseling for the them. We will sit with them one-on-one to talk about issues and problems. The organization also provides mentoring activities with the youths.

Support Group:

  • Stress management support group
  • Recession support group
  • Anger management (accept for court, business, and personal requirements)

Anger is a secondary emotion which is used to cope with feelings of vulnerability, pain, hurt, and fear. Anger is expressed inwards to depression, substance abuse, and cutting or outward to be physical and verbal aggression. Those coping with anger management issues have learned. They are victims of other behaviors and feel they must compensate and be the aggressor so they will not be re-victimized. Other times anger is expressed passively( passive aggressive behavior) or displaced (road rage)

Help or get help program:

The Somali Bantu youth is pleased to announce the Help or Get Help Program. It provides academic, social, cultural, and violence prevention support. The program will allow college, high school, middle school, and adult-education students to come together and get help with their school work, along with assistance with social cultural needs. The program will give the youth a chance to get help with their school work or offer them the chance to tutor others with school work. We will also do other school or cultural related activities. Local volunteers will be available to help the youths with their school work. If you have any questions or want to participate in these programs, pleases don’t hesitate to contact us.
