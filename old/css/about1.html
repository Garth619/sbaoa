Hello,

My name is Said-osman Abiyow and I am the founder of the Somali Bantu Association of America. I am a Somali Bantu and came to the United States in the early 1990’s as a child. It is my goal to help Somali Bantu refugees adjust to the American way of life by providing services and resources for my people, as well help resettle Somali Bantu into the U.S.

My biggest concern at the moment, is the language barrier that exists for Somali Bantu in America today. The problem with America’s view of Somali refugees is that they think it is one country, one religion, and one language. This is a myth. Somalia is full of many diverse communities. There are two langauges that the Somali Bantu speak, May May and Kizugua. Before the civil war, the minority groups wre excluded from oppurtunities of resettling in the United States. The dominant groups however, have been living in the United States for 10-15 years. The dominant groups of Somali do not speak the Bantu languages and cannot represent the Bantu effectively because they are a different group and need their own indepent representation. We are requesting the United States government hire May May and Kizugua speakers to translate for our people in various positions throughout society. The dominant Somali groups cannot do this. It can be dangerous if language barriers prevent medical help, court appointments, etc.

It has been a long and hard road from the refugee camps to acclimating to the norms of life in the United States. My wish is that this organization can help Somali Bantu adjust as they come to the United Staes. Thank you for helping us to continue our programs in order to help our community become self-sufficient and productive citizens of this wonderful country.

Sincerely



Said-osman Abiyow
