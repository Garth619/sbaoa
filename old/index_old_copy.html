Mission Statement

Our mission is to facilitate the resettlement of the Somali Bantu and to integrate cultural and life skill training programs within the community. We seek to promote English literacy, promote awareness of American culture, and encourage self-sufficiency among the Somali Bantu community in the United States, especially in San Diego.

Somali Bantu Refugees Travel to America's Heartland

In the 1880s Bantu tribe’s people were torn from their homelands in southern Africa and shipped to Somalia as slaves. Always targets of discrimination, they disproportionately fell victims to violence when the Somali government disintegrated in 1991. Even in refugee camps they were persecuted by fellow Somalis — robbed, raped, and forced into hard labor. Repatriation attempts to their original homeland failed.

The U.S. State Department granted asylum to 15,000 Bantu Somalis, paving the way for this Somali Bantu tribe to leave behind forever the Kenyan refugee camps where they have languished for a dozen years. With this announcement, one of the most primitive civilizations on earth prepared to assimilate into one of the most advanced.

The Somali Bantu Association of America, located in San Diego, CA, is a nonprofit organization committed to the effective integration of Somali Bantu and other refugee communities, to the United States, through the provision of social and economic programs. Somali Bantu refugees have been arriving in the United States since 1991 after spending more than a decade in Kenyan’s Refugee Camps or other peaceful countries. Prior to war in Somali regions, the Somali Bantu people suffered discrimination and economic marginalization in the early 1990’s.

We are the Somali Bantu Association of America, and we are requesting supportive help from the United States Government. Our goal is to help all refugees as it relates to Housing, Education, Vocational training, and U.S. Employment services. Basically we need help!

If you are interested in supporting us in any way by volunteering time, services, and getting involved in any of our programs, we would love to hear from you! Anything you can share with us will help, and will surely be appreciated.
